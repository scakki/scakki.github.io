Shivayogi Akki

Ph.D. student, Michigan Technological University

Hi! I'm a Ph.D. student in Electrical Engineering at Michigan Technological University, where I specialise in robotics, controls and reinforcement learning. I focus on comparative studies of Model Predictive Control (MPC) and Reinforcement Learning (RL) controllers for quadrupedal robots. My work explores the intersection of machine learning and robotics—if it moves and learns, I want to study it.

When I'm not debugging why my simulation turned into a somersaulting robot, you'll probably find me programming PLCs, working on industrial automation or developing autonomous navigation systems. Whether it's teaching robots to walk or optimising control algorithms, I'm all in for the learning, the challenges and the occasional victory when everything works as intended.

Industrial Assembly Line HIL Simulation

March 2023 – April 2023 • Hardware‑in‑the‑Loop Project

Created a Simulink model for a hybrid electric vehicle and implemented it in a Woodward controller. Used Hardware‑in‑the‑Loop testing to generate test signals and assess the blending strategy of the hybrid vehicle for performance and efficiency.

Electronic Throttle Valve with CAN Communication

Aug 2022 – Dec 2022 • Automotive Control Project

Developed a discrete PID controller and calibrated gains for precise control of an electronic throttle system using a DC motor. Established CAN communication between two electronic control units using a model‑based design approach and achieved approximately 95 % accuracy in throttle and pedal position measurements.

PID Control CAN Protocol Model‑Based Design DC Motor Control ECU Programming
